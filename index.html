Patrick McColl - Sportscaster

About Me

My name is Patrick McColl. I am a student at the University of Miami studying broadcast journalism, with a minor in sports administration.

Play-by-Play

With a driving passion to announce sports, I chose the University of Miami to pursue my beginnings in the space of play-by-play.

Since participating with WVUM 90.5 FM, I have been fortunate to call Hurricanes football, men’s and women’s basketball, baseball, volleyball, and soccer. Getting the chance to call these games has been a privilege and incredibly fun.

Recently, the Quincy Gems of the Prospect League gave me the chance to broadcast all Gems home games on Prospect League Television for the 2023 season. In the role, I broadcasted each game solo, giving me a mass of experience in the wonderful game of baseball.

On the Camera

Working in front of the camera is a necessity in the realm of television. Through experiences with UMTV, WGEM, and other works, I have grown comfortable in front of the lens.

Behind the Camera

Telling visual stories has always been a passion of mine. While in Miami, I have worked on television packages, shorter voice over segments, and other works.

The television work has not just been allocated for class, but the University of Miami’s television show “SportsDesk.” While in Quincy with the Gems, I assisted WGEM TV, the local NBC affiliate and television station. The position allowed me to gain more experience with tighter deadlines and real-world newsgathering for broadcast.

Amongst the bigger projects, I helped create a documentary over the course of a semester in Miami Beach. Covering an important race for the Miami Beach commissioner role, I learned more about the flexibility and time management skills necessary to perform a media project over a longer period.

At Miami, I worked in behind the scenes television production for both UMTV shows SportsDesk and NewsVision. In combination with classwork, I have filled the seats of many positions in the studio including director, technical director, server operator, camera operator, audio engineer, floor manager, teleprompter, and more.

Writing

I have a strong passion for storytelling, and sports are an incredibly useful medium for helping me pursue this passion. After beginning writing for “The Corral” at Parkway Central High School, I expanded my sports writing experience to Miami, writing for the school newspaper “The Miami Hurricane.” In addition, I currently write for the website FanBuzz.com.

During the spring semester of 2023 I wrote game recaps of several professional sporting events in Miami, being trained by one of the best, longtime sports reporters of the Miami Herald. While on deadline, I had the privilege of writing a game story for a Miami Heat game, a Miami Marlins game, an Inter Miami CF game, and a tennis match story at the Miami Open.
